üåç Current Research Projects 

<h4>Satellite-Based Irrigation Mapping in Sub-Saharan Africa</h4>
<p><strong>Collaborators:</strong> UCSB Earth Research Institute, Local Agricultural Extension Services</p>
<p><strong>Timeline:</strong> 2023-2025</p>

<p>This project uses high-resolution satellite imagery and machine learning to identify and map small-scale irrigation systems across Sub-Saharan Africa. Traditional remote sensing methods often miss these smaller agricultural practices, leading to significant underestimates of water use and agricultural productivity.</p>

<p><strong>Key Innovation:</strong> Developed a custom CNN architecture that combines Sentinel-2 imagery with local knowledge from farmers to achieve 94% accuracy in identifying irrigation patterns. The model is trained on hand-labeled data from local agricultural experts who understand their land better than any algorithm.</p>

<p><strong>Impact:</strong> This research is being used by local governments and NGOs to better understand water resource management and plan for climate adaptation strategies.</p>

<div style="background: rgba(255,255,255,0.2); padding: 1rem; border-radius: 8px; margin: 1rem 0;">
  <strong>üìä Technical Details:</strong> Using a combination of Sentinel-2 (10m resolution) and Planet imagery (3m resolution), with a custom U-Net architecture optimized for multi-temporal analysis. The model processes 12-month time series to capture seasonal irrigation patterns.
</div>
<h4>Machine Learning for Biodiversity Corridor Identification</h4>
<p><strong>Collaborators:</strong> UCSB Ecology Department, Local Conservation Organizations</p>
<p><strong>Timeline:</strong> 2024-2026</p>

<p>This project develops machine learning models to identify and map wildlife corridors using satellite imagery and species movement data. The goal is to help conservation organizations prioritize land protection efforts and design more effective wildlife corridors.</p>

<p><strong>Key Innovation:</strong> Integrating satellite imagery with citizen science data and GPS tracking data to create comprehensive habitat connectivity models. The approach combines landscape ecology principles with deep learning to predict optimal corridor locations.</p>

<p><strong>Impact:</strong> Results are being used by conservation organizations to guide land acquisition strategies and inform policy recommendations for wildlife protection.</p>
<h4>Indigenous Land Rights and Satellite Monitoring</h4>
<p><strong>Collaborators:</strong> Indigenous Land Rights Organizations, Legal Aid Groups</p>
<p><strong>Timeline:</strong> 2024-2025</p>

<p>This collaborative project uses satellite imagery to monitor land use changes on indigenous territories and provide evidence for land rights claims. The research combines remote sensing with community-based monitoring and legal documentation.</p>

<p><strong>Key Innovation:</strong> Developed participatory mapping protocols that combine satellite imagery with traditional ecological knowledge and community observations. The approach ensures that indigenous communities have access to the same technological tools as governments and corporations.</p>

<p><strong>Impact:</strong> Satellite imagery has been used as evidence in several land rights cases, helping indigenous communities protect their territories from illegal logging and mining.</p>
